Frequently Asked Questions

Do I need to mark utility lines before stump grinding?

Before any stump grinding work begins, customers must call 811.

  • ✓ Please note that North Carolina 811 requires 3 business days before your scheduled service. We will need customers ticket number from 811
  • ✓ Tony is not responsible for damage to unmarked underground utilities
  • ✓ This protects both you and our equipment from costly damage

How deep can your equipment grind?

Our professional-grade stump grinding equipment can grind stumps from 6 inches to 24 inches deep - significantly deeper than most competitors in Union County and Mecklenburg County.

  • ✓ Standard grinding depth and pricing: 6 inches or less below ground level is sufficient to eliminate hazards, improve aesthetics, and prevent from regenerating, and best for landscaping, where you plan to grow grass or small shrubs.
  • ✓ Maximum grinding depth: Up to 24 inches for large stumps, best to provide ample soil depth for replanting a new tree in the same area and substantial landscaping needs.
  • ✓ Grinding width: Unlimited ability
  • ✓ Deep enough for new landscaping, grass planting, or construction
  • ✓ Remote control precision technology ensures accurate depth control

Yes! The wood chips produced during stump grinding are yours to keep, and there are several easy ways to dispose of them or put them to good use.

  • ✓ Use as natural mulch around trees and flower beds
  • ✓ Landscaping companies often want free wood chips
  • ✓ Some customers spread them in wooded areas of their property
